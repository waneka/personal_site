beliefs

I believe in clear, easy to read code that makes sense to everyone and anyone that might see it.
I believe in modular design. The art of transforming data as it moves through the app and the practice of decoupling everything.
I believe that collaboration is key to good engineering; two heads are always better than one.
I believe in testing; good code shouldn't exist outside of a good test suite.
I believe that more important than learning things is the art of learning how to learn.

about me

I am a maker at heart. Whether it's building thoughtful web applications or crafting leather wallets, I'm at my best when I'm creating. There's nothing more rewarding to me than building something meaningful from scratch. In craftsmanship, persistence and tenacity are key attributes to success.

When I'm not behind a computer, you can probably find me spending time with my wonderful wife and golden retriever. I also love brewing (and drinking) beer, running through Golden Gate Park, barbecuing with friends, and exploring the incredible city of San Francisco. I find endless inspiration from the music library of Sufjan Stevens. I will never ever turn down a good cup of coffee. Ever.

my work

a little of what i've done:

qd.fm

qd.fm - the collaborative party playlist app that queues songs based on user votes. Partygoers can choose from rdio's library of over 20 million songs from their mobile device to the queue. The highest voted songs are played with priority while less popular songs remain at the bottom of the list ensuring no party ever dies on account of the playlist (though we can't be held accountable for the inevitable Rick Roll). Created from the ground up as a final project at Dev Bootcamp in October 2013.
